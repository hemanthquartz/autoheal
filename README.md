Topic: Roles in meeting

Here’s exactly what you can say (keep it simple + structured so you sound confident):

⸻

🔹 Start with a short intro (10–15 sec)

“Hi everyone, since I joined remotely, I’ll cover the roles in an effective meeting and how they help improve outcomes.”

⸻

🔹 Key Roles in a Meeting

1. Facilitator / Leader
	•	Guides the meeting
	•	Keeps discussion on track
	•	Ensures everyone participates

👉 “This role is critical to avoid meetings going off-topic.”

⸻

2. Timekeeper
	•	Makes sure agenda stays within time
	•	Gives reminders when time is running out

👉 “This helps meetings stay efficient and respectful of everyone’s time.”

⸻

3. Note Taker (Recorder)
	•	Captures key decisions and action items
	•	Shares meeting summary later

👉 “Without this role, important decisions can get lost.”

⸻

4. Participants / Contributors
	•	Share ideas and feedback
	•	Stay engaged and prepared

👉 “Active participation is what actually makes the meeting valuable.”

⸻

5. Decision Maker (if applicable)
	•	Final authority on decisions
	•	Helps avoid delays

👉 “This prevents meetings from ending without clear outcomes.”

⸻

🔹 How roles improve effectiveness (important question)

You can say:
	•	“Roles create structure and clarity”
	•	“They reduce confusion about who does what”
	•	“They help meetings stay focused and productive”
	•	“They ensure accountability for actions after the meeting”
